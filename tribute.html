Sushma Swaraj

Former Minister Of External Affairs Of INDIA

14 February 1952 – 6 August 2019

Here's a time line of Sushma Swaraj's life:

  • Sushma Sharma was born on 14 February in Ambala Catonment,Punjab,India which is now situated in Haryana,India.
  • In She was educated at Sanatan Dharma College in Ambala Cantonment and earned a bachelor's degree with majors in Sanskrit and Political Science. She studied law at Panjab University, Chandigarh
  • By 1970s She started her political career with Akhil Bharatiya Vidyarthi Parishad in the 1970s.
  • Swaraj started practice as an advocate in the Supreme Court of India.
  • Sushma Swaraj became a part of George Fernandes's legal defence team in 1975.She actively participated in Jayaprakash Narayan's Total Revolution Movement.
  • She was a member of the Haryana Legislative Assembly, winning the Ambala Cantonment assembly seat at the age of 25 and then, again from 1987 to 1990
  • In July,she was sworn in as a Cabinet Minister in the Janata Party Government headed by then Chief Minister Devi Lal.
  • She was Education Minister of Haryana state in the Bharatiya Janata Party–Lok Dal coalition government.
  • She served as Union Cabinet Minister for Information and Broadcasting during the 13-day government of PM Atal Bihari Vajpayee in 1996.
  • Became The Chief Minister of Delhi, but resigned, the same year .
  • Became Minister of Information and Broadcasting till 2004 then became the Minister of Health & Family Welfare .
  • Swaraj was re-elected to the Rajya Sabha for a third term in April 2006 from Madhya Pradesh state. She served as the Deputy leader of Opposition in Rajya Sabha till April 2009. .
  • She won the 2009 election for the 15th Lok Sabha from the Vidisha Lok Sabha constituency in Madhya Pradesh by the highest margin of over 400,000 votes .
  • Became the Minister of External Affairs, and was the second women at this place after Indra Gandhi,she took very crucial decisions for betterment of nation in worlds diplomacy.
  • This gem left the world in tears on 30 may, she died due to Cardiac Arrest .
  • She was awarded the Padma Vibhushan, India's second highest civilian award posthumously in 2020 in the field of Public Affairs.
